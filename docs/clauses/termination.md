# Termination

1. If the rent or any part of it remains unpaid for fourteen (14) days after its due date (whether formally demanded or not), or if the Tenant commits a material breach of this Agreement and fails to remedy it within fourteen (14) days of written notice, the Landlord may re-enter the Premises and this Agreement shall terminate, without prejudice to the Landlord's other rights and remedies.
2. If the Premises are destroyed or rendered unfit for habitation by fire, flood, or other cause not attributable to the Tenant, either party may terminate this Agreement by written notice, and rent shall be apportioned to the date of termination.
3. If the Landlord is in material breach of this Agreement and fails to remedy the breach within fourteen (14) days of written notice from the Tenant, the Tenant may terminate this Agreement by written notice, without prejudice to the Tenant's other rights and remedies.
4. Termination of this Agreement does not affect any obligation which accrued before termination.
